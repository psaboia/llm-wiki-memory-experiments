# Source: The Salinity-Drift Finding (results summary)

The Salinity-Drift Finding is the Tidewatch project's central result: tidal
drift velocity predicts near-surface salinity with high confidence across the
Coral Shelf. The finding uses Tidewatch buoy data transmitted over DriftNet and
calibrated with the Kelp Calibration method. It directly challenges the older
Bloom Hypothesis, which attributed the same salinity variation to algal blooms
rather than tidal drift. The Marren Lab considers the Bloom Hypothesis weakened
but not yet refuted.
