# Source: Kelp Calibration Method (methods appendix)

Kelp Calibration is a field procedure for correcting salinity sensors against a
known reference solution before deployment. It was created by the Marren Lab and
is the calibration step required by the DriftNet protocol. The Salinity-Drift
Finding relied on Kelp Calibration to rule out sensor drift as an artifact.
Kelp Calibration takes about twenty minutes per buoy.
